Algorithmic Foundations
of Data Science

We study algorithms with provable guarantees — approximation, privacy, and efficiency — often for problems where the data itself is uncertain, sensitive, or massive. Our work is theoretical, but the problems are drawn from machine learning and data science.

Meet the lab Publications

Research

What we work on

Our work sits at the intersection of algorithm design, data science, and theoretical computer science. We care about provable correctness and efficiency, not just empirical performance.

🔒

Differential Privacy

Algorithms that answer queries and learn from data with formal privacy guarantees. We design locally private models and solve private graph problems.

📐

Approximation Algorithms

Efficient algorithms with provable approximation ratios for hard combinatorial problems, including clustering, facility location, and correlation clustering.

📊

Learning Theory & Optimization

Providing theoretical foundations of machine learning, such as min-max optimization, stochastic methods, and low-rank approximation with convergence guarantees.

🕸️

Algorithmic Graph Problems

Graph algorithms with rigorous guarantees, from distance computation under privacy constraints to structural problems on large networks.

📍

Computational Geometry

Algorithms for geometric problems with imprecise or uncertain input, including Fréchet distance, color-spanning sets, and convex hull computation.
